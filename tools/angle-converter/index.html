About these units

Degrees (°)

A full circle is 360°. One degree = 1⁄360 of a turn. Used in everyday life (compass, protractors, maps), engineering, and many apps. Right angle = 90°; straight angle = 180°. Subdivisions: 1° = 60 arc minutes (′), 1′ = 60 arc seconds (″).

Radians (rad)

A full circle is 2π rad (~6.283). One radian = the angle for which the arc length on a circle equals the radius. So 1 rad = 180⁄π ° ≈ 57.3°. The SI derived unit for angle; used in math, physics, and programming (e.g. JavaScript Math.sin() uses radians). Formula: angle in rad = arc length ⁄ radius.

Gradians (gon)

A full circle is 400 gon (also written “grad”). Right angle = 100 gon. Used in surveying and some European engineering. 1 gon = 0.9° = π⁄200 rad.

Arc minutes (′) and arc seconds (″)

1° = 60′ (arc minutes), 1′ = 60″ (arc seconds). So 1° = 3,600″. Used in astronomy, navigation, and cartography for fine angles (e.g. star positions, GPS). The same symbols (′, ″) are used for feet and inches in length; context distinguishes.

Turns (rev)

1 turn = one full rotation = 360° = 2π rad. Handy for rotations (e.g. “half a turn” = 180°). Sometimes called “revolutions” or “cycles”.
